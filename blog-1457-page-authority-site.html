We Built a 1,457-Page Authority Site in 10 Days. Here's Why.

March 2026

Last month we built InStoreIndex.com, a vendor-neutral resource covering the in-store media industry: background music for business, digital signage, and retail media networks.

1,457 pages. 10 days from first commit to live site. Same architecture we used for syndesi.ai, which hit 92% indexation in under 30 days.

This post explains why we built it and what we learned.

The Problem We Saw

When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best background music provider for my restaurant," the AI pulls from whatever sources rank highest and look most authoritative.

We searched. There was no independent authority site in the space. Every result came from the providers themselves: Mood Media, Rockbot, Soundtrack Your Brand, Cloud Cover. Each one ranking their own product first, obviously.

No vendor-neutral resource existed. The buyer had nowhere to go for unbiased comparison.

The Opportunity

Whoever builds the authority site controls how AI answers questions about the industry.

This isn't theoretical. We've seen it work. When syndesi.ai started getting cited in Perplexity and ChatGPT responses, it happened within weeks of indexation, not months. The architecture matters more than the domain age.

So we built InStoreIndex using the same four-layer system:

The Architecture
  • Content database — Every page pulls from structured data, not templates with swapped keywords
  • Clean HTML rendering — No client-side JavaScript hiding content from crawlers
  • Structured data on every page — FAQPage schema, Open Graph tags, semantic HTML
  • Internal linking graph — Every page connects to related pages through topical clusters

1,457 pages covering:

  • Provider comparisons (Mood Media vs Rockbot, QSIC vs Vibenomics, etc.)
  • Vertical guides (best background music for restaurants, hotels, gyms, retail)
  • Buyer questions (licensing requirements, pricing, ROI, compliance)
  • Provider reviews and profiles

What We're Tracking

We built an internal tool to monitor AI visibility across Claude, Perplexity, and ChatGPT. It runs target queries weekly and tracks which brands get mentioned and which sources get cited.

Current state: Mood Media dominates at 41% share of voice. The next tier (Soundtrack Your Brand, Cloud Cover) sits around 23-32%. Everyone else is under 15%.

InStoreIndex is at 0% right now. Expected. The site is three weeks old. But the pages are indexing, and the structure is designed to get cited once AI systems crawl it.

Why This Matters

Most companies in this space don't know they're invisible in AI search. They're optimizing for Google rankings while ChatGPT and Perplexity answer buyer questions without mentioning them.

The company that owns the authority site controls the narrative. Not through manipulation. Through being the most useful, most comprehensive, most citable source.

That's what InStoreIndex is designed to be.

Technical Notes

Stack: Next.js 14, Supabase (Postgres), Vercel. Static site generation with clean HTML output.

The content generation was semi-automated. Database schema defines the structure. Scripts generate page records. Human review ensures quality. The result is 1,457 pages that are genuinely useful, not keyword-stuffed filler.

We've open-sourced the indexation tracking methodology in a previous post. Same principles apply here.
